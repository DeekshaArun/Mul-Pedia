-ABOUT-


India is the pioneer in producing Mulberry raw silk since the time immemorial and is blessed with salubrious climatic conditions suitable for growing mulberry crops throughout the year. Sericulture encompasses mulberry crops, silkworm and cocoon as its main components and these are interdependent on each other for the production of qualitative quantity of raw silk. Mulberry, an outstanding bio-energy food plant, requires optimum climatic conditions for its growth and development. Mulberry leaves with balanced nutrition is essential for bio mass production and to feed the silkworm. Majority of the farmers hardly pay attention to the nature of soil and abiotic factors due to which leaves become malnutritious and thus when they are consumed by the silkworm, desired quality end-product, raw silk, will not be produced.


-FACTORS-


The factors which affect the growth of healthy Mulberry leaves are soil quality parameters and environmental factors. The soil quality parameters namely, pH, Electrical conductivity (EC ds/m2 ), Organic carbon (OC%), Average Nitrogen (N Kg/ha), Available Phosphorus (P Kg/ha), Available Pottassium (K Kg/ha), Available Sulphur (S ppm), Average Boron (B ppm) and other micro nutrients aling with environmental factors influence the quality and quantity of Mulberry leaf yield. Soil pH basically is an important soil parameter and the availability of all other nutrients in the soil are dependent on the soil pH because, it controls the chemical processes. Organic Carbon content, also an important parameter, improves the morpho-physico-chemical properties of soil. The macronutrients responsible for the growth of Mulberry crops are Nitrogen, Phosphorous and Potassium (NPK) and these should necessarily be present in the soil in required levels. Nitogen is the main component of many structural, genetic (RNA and DNA) and metabolic compounds in the cells and one of the components of chlorophyll, which is used during photosynthesis. Phosphorous, another macronutrient, is required for plant growthand is responsible for energy transfer, photosynthesis, transformation of sugars and starches, nutrient movement within the plant and transfer of genetic characteristics from one generation to the next. Potassium is next important macronutrient which is present in the vacuole and cytoplasm without forming organic matter in cells and is responsible for the plant metabolism and helps in prevention of diseases, pests etc.

Other micronutrients such as Sulphur, Boron, Manganese, etc., which are required may be present in the soil, or is given to the plants using foliage spray which is sprayed on the leaves and therefore they enter through the stomata of the leaves. This is because these micronutrients get eroded when present in the soil and is therefore sprayed depending on their availability in the soil. All these soil parameters along with suitable environmental conditions are reponsible for the production of qualitative quantity of Mulberry crops.
